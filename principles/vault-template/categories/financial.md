# Financial

Money posture: how I think about earning, saving, giving, risk, and the lifestyle creep that comes with rising income.

This category is sneaky-important. Money decisions are downstream of beliefs about [[categories/career-professional|career]], [[categories/faith-spirituality|faith]], and [[categories/relationships|relationships]] — but they're also where principles are most easily ignored if not written down. Lifestyle creep happens silently.

## Prompts for the discovery session

- What % of income do I save / invest / give — and why those numbers?
- What's my rule for spending decisions above $X?
- What lifestyle level am I deliberately not crossing, even when I could?
- When risk shows up (job change, investment, business), what's the test?
- What's my generosity rule — recurring giving, situational giving, both?
- What money decisions have I made in the past that I'd repeat? Which would I not?
