# A meeting is not a substitute for the actual work — and I keep at least one morning a week protected for the actual work

> If the calendar is full and nothing got built, I worked 0 hours that week. Meetings are part of the job; they are not the job.

## Why it matters

I notice that when I have weeks with 30 hours of meetings and 10 hours of "execution time," the execution time gets eaten by recovery from the meetings. The work that compounds — writing, building, thinking through hard problems — only happens in unbroken blocks. Without an explicit principle protecting at least one morning per week, the calendar fills and that block disappears. This isn't about being anti-meeting; it's about being honest that meetings produce decisions, not artifacts. Decisions without artifacts are vapor.

## Source / Inspiration

- **External anchor:** Cal Newport's *Deep Work* — specifically the argument that knowledge work has bimodal value (shallow work has linear payoff, deep work has compounding payoff).
- **Personal experience:** Tracked time for six weeks in late 2024. The weeks where I shipped something I was proud of all had at least 4 hours of unbroken morning work. The weeks where nothing shipped had zero.

## Examples

- **Lived it:** Every Wednesday morning since November 2025 has been blocked. I've moved exactly two of them in five months — both for genuinely irreplaceable conversations.
- **Didn't live it:** December 2024 was a 4-week stretch where I "couldn't" hold the morning block because of year-end review cycles. Looking back, three of those mornings would have been fine to hold; I just didn't push back.
- **Upcoming:** the next two weeks have travel and a big client kickoff — I need to figure out which morning I'm holding before someone else fills it.

## Tensions / Where I struggle with this

- The cost is visible (someone wanted that time; I said no), the benefit is invisible (the thing I built in that time wouldn't be obviously connected to the time block in the future). Hard to defend.
- Some weeks the "deep work" turns into "stare at the screen and feel guilty" — I haven't fully figured out the difference between protecting the block and protecting it well. Suspect the answer is having a specific question or artifact targeted before the block starts.
- I struggle to apply this to relationships — protecting a morning block for my partner the same way I protect one for work feels selfish, but the same compounding logic should apply. Open question.
